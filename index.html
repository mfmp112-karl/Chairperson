Chairperson — Orientation Guide

"Be shepherds of God's flock that is under your care, watching over them — not because you must, but because you are willing."

1 Peter 5:2 — Jack's Role Verse

Jack, you are stepping into the Chairperson role — and I want you to understand from the start what this is and what it is not. It is not an administrative title. Makena holds the operational engine. What you hold is the people — their spiritual health, their unity, and the prayerful atmosphere that everything else is built on.

This department can be logistically excellent and spiritually hollow. Your job is to make sure that never happens. You set the temperature. Read this guide carefully, and come back to it whenever you need a reminder of your lane.

Reporting Lines

Who You Report To

Directly Caleb — Overseer. Your primary accountability. He convenes the monthly check-in and is your first call on anything requiring oversight authority.
Paired With Makena — ChairLady. You lead together. Your lanes are distinct and equally important. They must never compete.

Who Looks to You

All department members look to you pastorally. In terms of direct oversight, you and Makena jointly hold:

  • Faith — Prayer Secretary reports directly to you
  • Gloria — Events and Communications Lead, pastorally
  • All general ushers — every one of them

Your Role

What You Do

In one sentence: you set the spiritual temperature of the department and ensure every person in it is seen, cared for, and growing.

Every Sunday

  • Serve on the floor as an active usher — your presence matters before service begins
  • Hold the pre-service tone; lead or open prayer before Sunday deployment if called upon
  • Be available to any usher who needs pastoral support during or after service

Every Month

  • Approve Faith's prayer theme before it is published to the group
  • Confirm the date and lead — or assign a leader for — the all-ushers prayer meeting
  • Lead the quarterly leaders-only prayer meeting (once per quarter)
  • Attend the monthly leadership check-in with Caleb and Makena
  • Approve Gloria's outing proposal for the month

Every Quarter

  • Approve the retreat concept — format, programme, and its pastoral dimension
  • Lead or co-lead the retreat spiritually
  • Review department health with Makena and Caleb at the quarterly check-in

Ongoing

  • Stay pastorally aware of every member — not just the leaders
  • Surface any welfare, conduct, or spiritual concern at the monthly check-in with Caleb and Makena
  • Support Makena when disciplinary conversations are necessary
